## The Story

The US and Iran have agreed an initial deal to end the war between their two countries. This agreement is due to be signed by the end of this week. It is not expected to contain many details, but will be an agreement for 60 days of further negotiations on topics such as Iran's nuclear weapons programme and opening the Strait of Hormuz to ships. During the conflict, over 3,000 people were killed in Iran alone, global oil prices have risen dramatically, while the Iranian government has stayed in place.

## News Headlines & Key Words

### full circle
> What was it all for? US-Iran agreement brings bitter rivals *full circle* — *CNN*

**Meaning**: come back to a similar situation after a series of events (回到原点)
**Example**: She's come **full circle** by managing the team she used to play for.

### spin
> The *spin* wars about who wins and loses in the US-Iran 'ceasefire' have begun — *ABC*

**Meaning**: present information in a way that influences how people think (舆论导向；曲解)
**Example**: The news website gave a positive **spin** to the government's latest press release.

### tacit
> Iran deal is *tacit* admission of strategic defeat by Trump administration — *Sky News*

**Meaning**: understood or implied without being stated openly (心照不宣的；默认的)
**Example**: Everyone at the party had a **tacit** understanding of what they should wear.

## Critical Thinking Questions
1. Why might governments prefer *tacit* admissions over explicit ones in diplomacy?
2. How does the framing of "full circle" shape our understanding of whether the conflict achieved anything?
3. Can "who won" in a conflict ever be objectively determined, or is it always a matter of "spin"?
