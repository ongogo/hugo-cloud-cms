## 📘 英语学习笔记：Talking about Hates

#### 1. 核心导读

在这个 BBC Learning English 的 Real Easy English 播客中，主持人 Neil 和 Georgie 进行了一场关于"讨厌的事"的轻松对话。这个播客专为较低水平的英语学习者设计（A2级别），通过真实而简单的英语对话，帮助学习者掌握表达厌恶、愤怒和挫败感的实用词汇和短语。

**In this BBC Learning English Real Easy English podcast, hosts Neil and Georgie have a conversation about things they hate. This podcast is designed for lower-level English learners (A2) to help them master practical vocabulary and phrases for expressing dislike, anger, and frustration.**

#### 2. 重点词汇释义

| 单词/短语 | 释义 | 中文释义 | 来源 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **can't stand (something)** | really don't like something | 受不了；实在不喜欢 | Podcast transcript |
| **annoying / irritating** | causing a slight feeling of anger or upset | 令人恼火的；令人烦躁的 | Podcast transcript |
| **annoyed / irritated** | slightly angry or upset | 恼火的；烦躁的 | Podcast transcript |
| **frustrating** | causing some anger because something is delayed or prevented | 令人沮丧的；令人挫败的 | Podcast transcript |
| **frustrated** | quite angry because you cannot do or complete something | 沮丧的；挫败感 | Podcast discussion |

#### 3. 精彩选段与解析

**[Concert Experience]**
> **Neil**: "I was trying to listen to the music. Lots of people around me were talking loudly, and I really hate that."
> 
> **Georgie**: "Oh. It's quite **annoying**, I guess, yeah."
> 
> **解析**：Neil 描述演唱会上周围人大声交谈让他无法听音乐，**annoying** 表示"令人恼火的"，指事情本身的性质。

**[Can't Stand - Littering]**
> **Neil**: "Do you know what I **can't stand**? I **can't stand** it when people leave litter or rubbish, especially in a beautiful, natural place."
> 
> **Georgie**: "Yeah, because it spoils the beautiful environment."
> 
> **解析**：**can't stand** 比 hate 语气更强，表示"实在受不了、无法忍受"。

**[DIY - Personal Hate]**
> **Neil**: "I really hate DIY. DIY is when you do repairs to your own house or flat. I'm really bad at it. And because I know I'm bad at it, when I do it and it's a failure, everything about it makes me hate it."
> 
> **解析**：DIY = Do It Yourself（自己动手修缮），Neil 因为不擅长而讨厌做这事。

**[Crowds - Frustrating vs Annoying]**
> **Georgie**: "I don't like going through crowds of people because again, I like to get to places quickly and if I can't move easily in a crowd that **frustrates** me. I find that **annoying** and **irritating**."
> 
> **Neil**: "Yeah, it's really **irritating**, isn't it?"
> 
> **Georgie**: "And it's strange because usually I'm quite a calm and relaxed person... But for some reason, in these situations, I get very **annoyed**. It makes me very **annoyed**."
> 
> **解析**：关键区别：
> - **frustrating/frustrated** = 因为无法完成某事、被阻碍而产生的挫败感
> - **annoying/annoyed** = 事情本身令人不悦、恼火
> - **irritating/irritated** = 类似 annoying，但更强调生理/心理上的不适感

**[Food Preferences]**
> **Neil**: "How about food? Is there any food that you **can't stand**?"
> 
> **Georgie**: "Actually, no. I eat most food and I really love food. When I was younger, I used to hate nuts for some reason. I didn't like the texture. But now I think I don't mind them so much."
> 
> **解析**：used to hate 表示过去讨厌，现在不介意；texture = 口感/质地。

**[Self-Reflection: What We Do That Others Hate]**
> **Neil**: "I've got kids and kids are very honest. And so my kids think I'm really **annoying** in the morning, especially if I ask them lots of questions when they're making their breakfast."
> 
> **Georgie**: "There's definitely things that people find **annoying** about me. I'm very forgetful with my possessions... I often leave bags, phones, jackets, umbrellas in restaurants and bars. And my friends and my sister get really **annoyed**... **Annoying** for everyone!"
> 
> **解析**：自我反思环节，展示 annoyed/annoying 的主动/被动用法区别。

**[Vocabulary Recap]**
> **Neil**: "Let's recap the vocabulary we've heard in this episode, starting with **can't stand**. If you **can't stand** something, you really, really don't like it."
> 
> **Georgie**: "The adjectives **annoying** and **irritating** describe something that makes you feel uncomfortable or angry when it happens."
> 
> **Neil**: "Georgie just gave the adjectives that describe the situation, but **annoyed** or **irritated** describe the way you feel. So, if you are **annoyed** or **irritated**, something has made you angry."
> 
> **Georgie**: "If you feel **frustrated**, you're **annoyed** because you can't do something. For example, if you're in a traffic jam, you can feel **frustrated** that you can't move somewhere quickly. The situation is **frustrating**."
> 
> **解析**：节目结尾的词汇总结清晰区分了 -ing（描述事物）和 -ed（描述感受）的形容词区别，以及 frustrating 的特定用法。

---

#### 4. 学习思考

1. **情感表达层级**：hate > can't stand > annoying/irritating > frustrating。尝试用这四个层级描述同一件事（如：堵车、排队、噪音）。
2. **-ing vs -ed 形容词**：为什么 annoying 描述事物，annoyed 描述人？试着造句区分：boring/bored, exciting/excited, tiring/tired。
3. **Frustrated 的独特性**：为什么 frustrated 专门指"想做却做不成"？回忆一次你感到 frustrated 的经历，用英语描述。
4. **文化差异**：中文里"烦"、"恼"、"气"、"急"如何对应 annoying/irritated/frustrated/angry？
5. **生活应用**：列出 3 件你 can't stand 的事，3 件让你 frustrated 的事，尝试用英语向语伴倾诉。

### 🎧 听力原文

**Neil**
Hello and welcome to Real Easy English. In this podcast, we have real conversations in easy English to help you learn. I'm Neil.

**Georgie**
And I'm Georgie. You can find a video version of this podcast with subtitles on our website, bbclearningenglish.com.

**Neil**
Hi, Georgie. How are you?

**Georgie**
I'm very well, thank you, Neil. How are you?

**Neil**
I'm OK. At the weekend, I went to a concert.

**Georgie**
Yes.

**Neil**
And I was trying to listen to the music. Lots of people around me were talking loudly, and I really hate that.

**Georgie**
Oh. It's quite **annoying**, I guess, yeah. Well, today we're actually talking about things that we don't like – things that we hate doing and things that we don't like other people doing. So, let's talk about your concert then. How... why didn't you like that?

**Neil**
I just think it's... it feels quite rude to me because lots of people want to listen to the music and they paid money to listen to the music, but other people have decided just to talk and it means I couldn't listen to my favourite music.

**Georgie**
Yeah, and the tickets are expensive! And you go there for a reason and other people can ruin the experience for you. This happens also in the cinema or the theatre. Yeah, I find that **annoying** too. I don't like it when people… when groups of people are walking down the street in a line and they don't leave space for other people to pass. I can sometimes be a bit impatient, and maybe that's my problem, but yeah, that annoys me.

**Neil**
Do you know what I **can't stand**?

**Georgie**
What?

**Neil**
I **can't stand** it when people leave litter or rubbish, especially in a beautiful, natural place. So, if I'm in the countryside and then I see some old wrappers or...

**Georgie**
Cans.

**Neil**
...cans left lying on the ground, that makes me sad and angry.

**Georgie**
Yeah, because it spoils the beautiful environment. Is there anything that you hate doing personally?

**Neil**
Oh, lots of things. I really hate DIY. DIY is when you do repairs to your own house or flat.

**Georgie**
Yes.

**Neil**
I'm really bad at it. And because I know I'm bad at it, when I do it and it's a failure, everything about it makes me hate it.

**Georgie**
Another thing that I don't like doing is, similar to what I said before, I don't like going through crowds of people because again, I like to get to places quickly and if I can't move easily in a crowd that **frustrates** me. I find that **annoying** and **irritating**.

**Neil**
Yeah, it's really **irritating**, isn't it?

**Georgie**
And it's strange because usually I'm quite a calm and relaxed person and I just think, well, whatever happens happens. But for some reason, in these situations, I get very **annoyed**. It makes me very **annoyed**.

**Neil**
How about food? Is there any food that you **can't stand**?

**Georgie**
Actually, no. I eat most food and I really love food. When I was younger, I used to hate nuts for some reason. I don't like the texture but... or I didn't like the texture. But now I think I don't mind them so much. I just don't want nuts in my sweet treats. So, Neil, do you think that there's anything that you do that other people hate?

**Neil**
Oh, definitely. I've got kids and kids are very honest. And so my kids think I'm really **annoying** in the morning, especially if I ask them lots of questions when they're making their breakfast.

**Georgie**
I can really relate to that, to your kids actually, because I really don't like it when people ask me lots of questions in the morning. I'm just waking up. I don't want to talk to anyone at the moment. Anything else?

**Neil**
Well probably lots, but let's leave it there. How about you?

**Georgie**
There's definitely things that people find **annoying** about me. I'm very forgetful with my possessions – so, things that I own. I often leave bags, phones, jackets, umbrellas in restaurants and bars. And my friends and my sister get really **annoyed** because they have to follow me around making sure that I've got all of my things. And I think that's very **annoying** for them. It's **annoying** for me as well because I lose lots of things, like my phone a couple of times. **Annoying** for everyone!

**Neil**
OK. Let's recap the vocabulary we've heard in this episode, starting with **can't stand**. If you **can't stand** something, you really, really don't like it.

**Georgie**
The adjectives **annoying** and **irritating** describe something that makes you feel uncomfortable or angry when it happens.

**Neil**
Georgie just gave the adjectives that describe the situation, but **annoyed** or **irritated** describe the way you feel. So, if you are **annoyed** or **irritated**, something has made you angry.

**Georgie**
If you feel **frustrated**, you're **annoyed** because you can't do something. For example, if you're in a traffic jam, you can feel **frustrated** that you can't move somewhere quickly. The situation is **frustrating**.

**Neil**
That's it for this episode of Real Easy English, but don't forget to go to our website, bbclearningenglish.com, where you can download a free worksheet.

**Georgie**
And we'll be back next week with another conversation in Easy English. Goodbye for now.

**Neil**
Goodbye!
