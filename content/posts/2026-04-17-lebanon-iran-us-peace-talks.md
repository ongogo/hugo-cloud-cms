## English Summary
A temporary 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon, announced by US President Donald Trump, is seen as a crucial diplomatic step to enable a second round of Pakistan-mediated peace talks between Washington and Tehran. ## Article

A temporary ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon unveiled by US President Donald Trump on Thursday was the final turn in the diplomatic maze leading to a breakthrough in Pakistan-mediated peace talks between Washington and Tehran, analysts say.

Arranged by Pakistan with help from Saudi Arabia, the 10-day cessation of hostilities in Lebanon was a key Iranian condition for a second and potentially conclusive round of talks in Islamabad on a date yet to be set.

Marathon talks between high-powered delegations led by US Vice President J.D. Vance and Iranian parliamentary speaker Mohammed Bagher Ghalibaf in Islamabad last weekend stalled over Washington’s reluctance to include Lebanon on the agenda and Tehran’s refusal to cease its uranium enrichment activity, according to officials on both sides.

Israel reluctantly agreed to the ceasefire on the condition that its forces would remain in parts of southern Lebanon, which it has occupied since its war against Iran alongside the US was launched on February 28.

But it was “very unlikely” that the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire would last without progress in Islamabad, according to Hussein Ibish, an expert on the Levant and senior resident scholar of the Washington-based Arab Gulf States Institute.

The Israeli government “appears committed to a major war” with Hezbollah and was trying to “deliver a knockout blow” to the key Iranian ally. “They are taking a break now to facilitate US negotiations with Iran, but I think they’re keen to start the war again as soon as possible,” Ibish said.

Michael Young, senior editor of the Carnegie Middle East Institute in Beirut, said Hezbollah “does not really feel in any way committed” to the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire, which the militant group opposed.

“So if the Iranians and Americans don’t arrive at a ceasefire, it is certainly going to create tensions on the Lebanon-Israel front. These two negotiations have to advance in tandem,” Young said.

Trump announced the ceasefire after speaking to Lebanon’s President Joseph Aoun on Thursday after Aoun had baulked at the idea of talks with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu while his country was under Israeli fire.

Their conversation coincided with shuttle diplomacy by top Pakistani mediators. Field Marshal Asim Munir, the chief of defence forces, conveyed messages from Washington to Iran’s top civil and military leaders in Tehran, while Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif travelled to Saudi Arabia, Qatar and Turkey for talks with their leaders.

However, it was far from certain whether Lebanon’s weak and unstable government would benefit from peace between Washington and Tehran. Neither Iran nor its regional rivals – Turkey and the Gulf monarchies – would want Lebanon to fall under Israel’s sphere of influence.

Young foresaw growing tensions between Israel on one side and Turkey and Saudi Arabia on the other side over Lebanon. “Neither of them wants to see Israeli hegemony gain power through Lebanon,” he said.

## Phrases & Connectors 短语与连接词
1. **diplomatic maze** - 外交迷宫 - 形容极其复杂且难以突破的外交局势
 - *Context: \"...was the final turn in the diplomatic maze leading to a breakthrough...\"*
2. **cessation of hostilities** - 停火 / 停止敌对行动 - 正式的军事/外交用语
 - *Context: \"...the 10-day cessation of hostilities in Lebanon was a key Iranian condition...\"*
3. **stalled over** - 因为...而陷入停滞 - 描述谈判在某一点上无法达成一致
 - *Context: \"...in Islamabad last weekend stalled over Washington’s reluctance to include Lebanon on the agenda...\"*
4. **deliver a knockout blow** - 给予致命一击 - 原指拳击，此处指彻底摧毁敌方力量
 - *Context: \"...and was trying to 'deliver a knockout blow' to the key Iranian ally.\"*
5. **advance in tandem** - 同步推进 - 描述两个相关的过程必须同时进展
 - *Context: \"These two negotiations have to advance in tandem.\"*
6. **baulk at the idea of** - 对...的想法感到犹豫/拒绝 - 表示不愿接受某种提议
 - *Context: \"...after Aoun had baulked at the idea of talks with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu...\"*
7. **shuttle diplomacy** - 穿梭外交 - 外交官在多个国家之间往返以调停冲突
 - *Context: \"Their conversation coincided with shuttle diplomacy by top Pakistani mediators.\"*
8. **fall under [someone's] sphere of influence** - 落入某人的势力范围 - 描述国家在政治/军事上受到强权的控制
 - *Context: \"Neither Iran nor its regional rivals... would want Lebanon to fall under Israel’s sphere of influence.\"*
